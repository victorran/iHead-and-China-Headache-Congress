Dear Colleague,

On behalf of the Organizing Committees of the iHEAD Academy and the 2026 China Headache Congress (CHC), it is our great honor and pleasure to extend to you a warm welcome to Xi'an — the ancient imperial capital of China.

This year marks a historic milestone: for the first time, the International Headache Society's iHEAD Academy will be held in China, co-located with the annual China Headache Congress, in partnership with the International Headache Society (IHS) and the Chinese Headache Society (CHS). Together, these two premier meetings will bring together the world's leading headache experts and a new generation of clinicians for an unparalleled programme of education, dialogue, and discovery.

We are delighted to have you with us and look forward to making this a truly memorable academic and cultural experience.

Welcome to Xi'an — Ancient Capital of Thirteen Dynasties

A cultural crossroads of East and West for two millennia

Xi'an (西安) is one of the oldest cities in the world and the former capital of ancient China for over 1,100 years across thirteen dynasties — including the magnificent Han and Tang Empires. As the eastern starting point of the ancient Silk Road, Xi'an has been a cultural crossroads of East and West for more than two millennia. Today it is a vibrant metropolis of 13 million people that seamlessly weaves its extraordinary heritage into modern urban life.

Brief Descriptions

Terracotta Army (秦始皇兵马俑)

One of the greatest archaeological finds of the 20th century — 8,000+ life-sized warriors protecting Emperor Qin's tomb. A UNESCO World Heritage Site ~45 min from the city.

Xi'an City Wall (西安城墙)

The best-preserved ancient city wall in China, built in the Ming Dynasty (1370 AD). Rent a bicycle and cycle the full 13.7 km circuit for a breathtaking panorama.

Big Wild Goose Pagoda (大雁塔)

A Buddhist pagoda built in 652 AD to house sacred scriptures brought from India by monk Xuanzang. The adjacent Tang Paradise park hosts spectacular nightly fountain shows.

Shaanxi Cuisine — Must-Try Foods

Experience the authentic flavors of Xi'an

🍜

Yangrou Paomo (羊肉泡馍)

Xi'an's signature dish — a rich slow-cooked lamb broth served with hand-crumbled flatbread. A warming experience unique to this city.

🍜

Roujiamo (肉夹馍)

Often called the "Chinese hamburger" — slow-braised spiced pork in crispy flatbread. Available at every street corner.

🍜

Biang Biang Noodles (biang biang面)

Xi'an's famous hand-pulled belt noodles, tossed with chilli oil, vinegar, and toppings — the character 'biang' has over 57 strokes.

🍜

Liangpi (凉皮)

Chilled rice or wheat noodles in a tangy chilli sauce — a refreshing local snack perfect for May.

🥧

Persimmon Cake (黄桂柿子饼)

A traditional Xi'an dessert made from local persimmons, crispy outside and soft inside.

🥧

Osmanthus Rice Cake (桂花糕)

A sweet, fragrant pastry infused with osmanthus blossom — a perfect edible souvenir.

Suggested Itineraries

Make the most of your time in Xi'an

▶ Morning / Afternoon Option A — Ancient Wonders

Start at Terracotta Army (~45 min by taxi, ¥60). Allow 2–3 hours on-site. Return via Mount Li (Huaqing Palace hot springs) for lunch. Back in the city by evening.

▶ Morning / Afternoon Option B — City Centre Heritage

Walk from your hotel to the Bell Tower, explore the Muslim Quarter for breakfast, visit the City Wall (cycling recommended). End with tea at a traditional tea house near the Drum Tower.

▶ Evening Option — Tang Night at Da Ci'en Temple

The Big Wild Goose Pagoda North Square hosts a free water & music show every evening at 21:00. Combine with dinner at a nearby Shaanxi restaurant.

▶ Full-Day Option — Shaanxi History Museum + Famen Temple

Morning at Shaanxi History Museum (book ahead), afternoon drive to Famen Temple (~120 km, day-trip possible), home to the finger bone relic of the Buddha.
